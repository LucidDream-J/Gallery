New AI Masters?

Learning from the masters

Well if AI can claim something to be art then maybe it can "make" art ... eg Duchamp's urinal!

Duchamp was, Picasso aside, "the major influence" on art in the 20th century and even now ... he opened 'the can' even more.

After these two, anything could be art, chance events, the act of doing or making nothing could be perceived a work of art...Cage 4 minutes of silence ... de Traffa's empty canvases

In this context maybe an AI system can be seen to make art

artist as machine

the AI does not need to make any claims, be conscious, have general intelligence, be creative, have intention or emotion ... even if it could... and what's more AI is already always socially embedded... Warhol's "I am a machine"

in this context many already see some AI systems as "generating - making" "art" and exploit the possibilities? Maybe for anything or non-thing to be "art" is always a collaboration?

Additionaly is it Ai creates art or mohbe we can create Ai/s that are, accidently, or contextually designed to "make artin new ways", no matter whichever particular art or cultural theoretical perspectives are assumed / taken on "what is Art" or "what could be Art"?

Throughout history art has changed in terms of within any particular medium and in terms of any new media! At this point Ai, as it stands, appears another new media ...

Art in early 21st century

Contempory art is increasingly academic.

This together with the rise of groups who see themselves as our guides, gurus and protectors of social cultural behaviours, has led to ideological dogmatism, top down prescriptivism and generally listless and predictable art forms.

Artists working in this framework, on inspection turn out generally to do nothing more than give exposure to acnied theory and narratives found in any first year cultural studies course.

Their works protraying theoretical cliche, textualised lives, textualized experiences and textualised expression, which though reassuring to some, are limiting and oppressive to most.

In these cases acnied theorized models are taken as being lived reality.

Fortunately life does not exist simply within language, theory and text but rather language although critical is one aspect in life.

LIVING is diverse, involving pre-linguistic and extra-linguistic expreinces and preceptions, offering far more than preconcieved theoretics, scripts and narratives. Unless one is an automoron, which some arrogantly take people to be.

Instead of the disembodied mentalistic somewhat bad-faith practices there are alternatives, one of which is an embodied, embedded and a critically aware dynamic situated practice.

Artists try to show / communicate the un-sayable, the experienced un-defineable, the experienced un-"knowable" even in social life.

Dan Crossley
